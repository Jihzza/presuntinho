🎯 The Three Forces

1. Dupe Collapse

The Spanish dupe segment contracted from 37% to 13% of consumers between 2018 and 2023 — a 65% drop in Equivalenza's core market.

2. The Sandwich Effect

Premium brands discounting from above (more accessible). Zara and Mercadona improving from below (more aspirational). Equivalenza squeezed.

3. Digital Native Competitors

Divain, Dossier, Adopt — operating without physical overhead, investing more in marketing per euro of revenue.

🏢 Competitor: Divain

Why them: Most direct structural threat. Same price point (€15-30). Digital-first model.

Strengths

  • No physical retail overhead → lower costs, higher marketing investment per €
  • Clean contemporary visual identity appeals to younger consumers
  • Strong Instagram/TikTok influencer marketing
  • Agile product launches unconstrained by franchise alignment

Weaknesses

  • No physical store presence — eliminates the sensory experience
  • No refill model — missing recurring revenue mechanism
  • Customer acquisition heavily dependent on paid social (rising costs)

Course Theory

SWOT, TOWS, SCQA, Buyer Persona — the frameworks you need.

🔍 SWOT Analysis

Internal + external factors. Two-by-two: helpful vs harmful, internal vs external.

S — Strengths (Internal, Helpful)

528 stores, 2/3 margin, Les Secrets, refill model, Italy diversification

W — Weaknesses (Internal, Harmful)

30% awareness, 1% e-commerce, NPS 17, inconsistent franchise quality

O — Opportunities (External, Helpful)

Gen Z growth, Spanish category +10%/yr, sustainability trend, Les Secrets upmarket

T — Threats (External, Harmful)

Dupe segment −65%, sandwich effect, digital-native competitors

❓ SCQA Framework

Structure for framing a marketing problem.

  • S — Situation: the stable context (what's true today)
  • C — Complication: the disruption or tension
  • Q — Question: the strategic question it raises
  • A — Answer: the marketing problem statement

👥 Buyer Persona

A research-based archetype. Three layers:

  • Demographics: age, location, income, education
  • Psychographics: values, attitudes, lifestyle, identity
  • Behavioural: discovery channels, purchase process, frustrations, loyalty drivers

Pro tip: psychographics beats demographics in fragrance. Marta doesn't buy by age — she buys by mood and identity.

Assignment Walkthrough

What the professor wants in each section.

1️⃣ SWOT Analysis (20%)

What to do: SWOT for Equivalenza + 1 competitor (Divain).

How: 4-5 bullets per quadrant. Cite the case study (Elson, 2024). Be specific with numbers.

Hint: NPS 17 vs Druni 56 is a weakness that doubles as the bridge to your Marketing Problem.

2️⃣ Buyer Persona (20%)

What to do: One primary persona with demographics, psychographics, behavioural, frustrations.

How: Anchor in case-study research. Add a "Strategic Importance" paragraph explaining why this persona matters.

3️⃣ Marketing Problem (20%)

What to do: Frame the most urgent problem using SCQA.

How: Show your reasoning. End with a clear problem statement (1-2 sentences) that your recommendation will solve.

4️⃣ TOWS Matrix (20%)

What to do: 2x2 matrix. 4 strategic alternatives. Brief evaluation of each.

How: Each cell = 1 strategy in 1 line. Then a paragraph explaining why SO wins (or whichever you pick).

5️⃣ Strategic Recommendation (20%)

What to do: Pick one TOWS strategy and defend it.

How: Why this one (not the others). Feasibility check. Implementation plan with leadership, resources, timeline.

Module 5

Writing Tips & Anti-AI Detection

How to write Q3-Q5 that sound like you, not a bot.

✍️ Sentence Variation

The problem: AI text tends to have uniform sentence length and predictable transitions ("Moreover," "Furthermore," "Additionally").

The fix:

  • Alternate lengths: short punchy sentences. Then longer ones with subordination. Then medium.
  • Start differently: sometimes with "I," sometimes with a question, sometimes with a clause.
  • Avoid stacking 3+ "Moreover-Furthermore-Additionally" in a paragraph.

🗣️ Personal Voice

  • Use contractions: don't, won't, it's, we're
  • Have opinions: "I find this striking," "What stood out to me is..."
  • Add your reading: "This suggests..." or "The implication seems to be..."
  • Use first person where appropriate: "I would recommend..."

🚫 Buzzwords to Avoid

AI loves these. Humans use them sparingly:

  • "delve into," "tapestry," "vibrant," "robust," "leverage" (use "use")
  • "in conclusion," "in summary," "to summarize"
  • "It is important to note that..."
  • "navigate the complexities," "in today's fast-paced world"

💎 Specific Examples

Replace generic claims with specific case details:

  • ❌ "The brand has lost market share."
  • ✅ "Spanish retail sales dropped from €22M to €14M between 2018 and 2023."
  • ❌ "Customer satisfaction is low."
  • ✅ "An NPS of 17 places Equivalenza well below Druni (56) and Primor (46)."

🤔 Hedging

Strategic uncertainty makes text feel human:

  • "It seems that..."
  • "This might suggest..."
  • "One could argue..."
  • "Perhaps the most important factor is..."

Para estudares na tua língua materna.

📚 O que é um SWOT?

Análise SWOT é uma ferramenta de diagnóstico estratégico. Divide em 4 quadrantes:

  • S — Strengths (Forças): o que a empresa faz BEM internamente
  • W — Weaknesses (Fraquezas): o que faz MAL internamente
  • O — Opportunities (Oportunidades): tendências externas POSITIVAS
  • T — Threats (Ameaças): tendências externas NEGATIVAS

Exemplo Equivalenza: Forças = 528 lojas; Fraquezas = NPS 17; Oportunidades = crescimento Gen Z; Ameaças = colapso do segmento dupe.

🎯 O que é TOWS?

TOWS é a evolução do SWOT — cruza os quadrantes para gerar 4 TIPOS de estratégia:

  • SO (Ofensiva): usa Forças para Aproveitar Oportunidades → crescimento
  • WO (Corretiva): ultrapassa Fraquezas para Aproveitar Oportunidades → melhoria
  • ST (Defensiva-Ofensiva): usa Forças para Neutralizar Ameaças → proteção
  • WT (Defensiva): minimiza Fraquezas e evita Ameaças → contenção

❓ O que é SCQA?

Framework para enquadrar um problema de marketing de forma lógica:

  • S — Situation (Situação): o contexto estável atual
  • C — Complication (Complicação): o que mudou, a tensão
  • Q — Question (Questão): a pergunta estratégica
  • A — Answer (Resposta): a formulação do problema

👤 O que é uma Buyer Persona?

Uma personagem semi-ficcional baseada em investigação real que representa o teu cliente ideal. Três camadas:

  • Demografia: idade, localização, rendimento, educação
  • Psicografia: valores, atitudes, estilo de vida, identidade
  • Comportamental: como descobre, avalia, compra, mantém lealdade

💡 Dica final para escrever

Quando reescreveres o assignment na tua voz, lembra-te:

  • Usa contrações: não, é, estou, vamos
  • Opiniões pessoais: "eu recomendaria", "na minha leitura"
  • Hedging: "parece-me", "sugere que", "talvez"
  • Exemplos específicos do caso (não generalidades)
  • Varia o comprimento das frases
